How Much Should You Feed Your Cat or Dog?

Feeding your pet the right amount is one of the most important things you can do for their long-term health. Too much food leads to obesity, joint problems, and a shorter lifespan. Too little can cause nutritional deficiencies and low energy. This free cat and dog food calculator takes the guesswork out of pet feeding and pairs with our printable homemade pet treats guide and pet feeding cheat sheet.

What This Calculator Uses

Our calculator uses the scientifically recognized Resting Energy Rate (RER) formula, adjusted for your pet's life stage, breed, sex, activity level, and weight goal. It estimates daily calories, kibble in cups and grams, wet food in grams, and a daily water intake target.

Cat Feeding Guide

Adult cats typically need between 200 and 350 calories per day depending on weight and activity. Indoor neutered cats need fewer calories than outdoor or unneutered cats. Breeds like Bengal and Sphynx tend to have higher energy needs than Persian or British Shorthair cats.

Dog Feeding Guide

Dogs vary widely — a Chihuahua and a Great Dane have very different calorie needs. Large breeds, working dogs, and highly active dogs need significantly more food than small or low-activity dogs. Always adjust based on your dog's body condition score, not just the number on the bag.
